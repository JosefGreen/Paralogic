## Verified Public Anchor

The NIST page identifies the AI RMF as a framework for managing risks to
individuals, organizations, and society associated with artificial intelligence.
The page states that AI RMF 1.0 was released on January 26, 2023, that it is
intended for voluntary use, and that NIST is revising it. It also links to the
AI RMF 1.0 download and related playbook/resource materials.

## Bounded Claims This Source May Support After Page Extraction

1. NIST provides a public risk-management framework for AI systems that can
   support CALM's insistence on bounded AI claims.
2. AI risk management requires governance and evaluation practices rather than
   mere adoption labels.
3. AI risk guidance is evolving; CALM should treat current AI claims as
   freshness-sensitive and implementation-specific.

## Claims This Source Must Not Be Used To Prove

1. Any specific defense AI system is safe, unsafe, effective, or ineffective.
2. CALM is validated.
3. Compliance with NIST AI RMF proves mission reliability.
4. "AI-enabled" means trustworthy, governed, or operationally valid.
