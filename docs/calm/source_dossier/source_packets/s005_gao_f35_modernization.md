## Verified Public Anchor

The GAO page identifies GAO-24-106909 as a public report published on May 16,
2024. The public summary says the F-35 program moved into full-rate production
after final simulated tests, while contractors continued late engine and
aircraft deliveries. It also identifies Technology Refresh 3 and Block 4
modernization delays, unresolved modernization decisions, and lifecycle cost
estimates exceeding $2 trillion for development, maintenance, and operation of
the planned fleet through 2088.

## Bounded Claims This Source May Support After Page Extraction

1. F-35 program claims should distinguish baseline production status from
   delivery performance, modernization progress, software/hardware integration,
   supplier risk, and lifecycle sustainment burden.
2. Full-rate production or advanced capability language does not erase
   unresolved modernization and sustainment questions.
3. CALM can use the F-35 as a bounded example of why "real capability" and
   "unbounded readiness claim" are not the same thing.

## Claims This Source Must Not Be Used To Prove

1. The F-35 is a failed aircraft.
2. The F-35 is combat ineffective.
3. Contractors acted in bad faith.
4. GAO validates CALM.
5. Cost, delay, or modernization risk alone proves operational failure.
