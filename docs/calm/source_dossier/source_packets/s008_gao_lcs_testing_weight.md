## Verified Public Anchor

The GAO page identifies GAO-14-749 as a public report published on July 30,
2014. The public summary says the Navy continued testing LCS systems and
capability, but important questions remained about how LCS would operate and
what capabilities it would provide. It records that a first operational
deployment gave the Navy a chance to examine concepts but was limited to one
variant and one mission package, that mechanical problems reduced planned
operational time, and that key concepts and performance had not yet been fully
demonstrated in an operational environment. It also flags weight-management
and concurrency risks from buying ships while key concepts and performance were
still being tested.

## Bounded Claims This Source May Support After Page Extraction

1. LCS public oversight materials can support a bounded case about concurrency,
   operational demonstration, mission-package evidence, and weight-management
   risk.
2. Testing pieces of a system and deploying a limited configuration do not
   automatically establish whole-program mission validity.
3. Acquisition choices can preserve production momentum while leaving some
   operational questions unresolved.

## Claims This Source Must Not Be Used To Prove

2. LCS sailors, program officials, or contractors acted in bad faith.
3. The LCS concept was impossible in principle.
