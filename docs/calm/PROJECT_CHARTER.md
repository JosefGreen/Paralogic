# CALM / Paper Armies Project Charter

## Status

This is a Phase 0 control-baseline artifact for the CALM and Paper Armies
workstream. It is not a finished doctrine, not a finished manuscript, not an
evidence dossier, and not empirical validation.

## Project Identity

Paper Armies is the flagship public work.

Preferred subtitle: A Doctrine for Defense Reform in the Age of Speed, AI, and
Strategic Competition.

CALM means Capability Assurance and Learning Mobility.

The project studies how institutional representations can outrun operational
reality, and how valid local learning can fail to travel. It is a reform
doctrine for claim validity, learning transfer, and reflexive anti-theater
discipline inside complex defense and technology institutions.

## Public Posture

The author approaches this as a systems delivery professional trained in
project management and space studies, examining how smart, patriotic people can
become trapped in fragile incentive loops that reward clean claims more
reliably than operational truth.

Professional experience supplies the lens. Public, verifiable sources must
carry factual claims about institutions, programs, technologies, events, or
current conditions.

## Core Thesis

Modern defense institutions do not fail only because they are slow or
bureaucratic. A deeper failure appears when representations of capability move
with more authority than the validated capability they describe.

Paper capability is capability whose institutional claim exceeds demonstrated
operational validity.

A Paper Army is a force whose representations of capability become more mobile,
coherent, and trusted than validated capability itself, while valid local
learning cannot travel far enough to prevent reinvention.

CALM's operating line is:

Make false confidence harder to move. Make valid learning easier to reuse.

## Non-Negotiable Boundaries

This project must not become an expose, a whistleblower memoir, an
anti-contractor polemic, an anti-military book, or a complaint about one
employer. It attacks fragile systems, incentive loops, claim drift, and learning
confinement, not identifiable individuals.

Do not use employer-specific anecdotes. Do not imply representation of an
employer, client, agency, contractor, office, or program. Do not infer
classified, controlled, internal, or current-program information.

## Source Rules

Official sources carry factual burden wherever available. Federally funded
research and development centers and think tanks support policy interpretation
and strategic framing. Academic sources support theory and method. Journalism
may support current framing or human narrative when bounded carefully.

No primary chapter claim should rely on weak source maturity. Personal
experience may explain why the question matters, but not prove factual claims.

2. CALM Doctrine Manual.

4. Source and Case Dossier.

6. Method Integration Profiles.
7. Human Cost Source Packets.
8. Countercase Dossier.

10. Reflexivity Defense / Paper CALM audit system.

## Usefulness Check

This charter prevents the project from drifting into generic reform prose. It
sets the author firewall, product boundaries, factual-evidence rule, core
definitions, and completion criteria before drafting.

## Claim Boundary

This charter does not prove that CALM works, that Paper Armies is publishable,
or that any defense case supports the thesis. It defines the controlled baseline
from which those claims must later be sourced, drafted, tested, and reviewed.
