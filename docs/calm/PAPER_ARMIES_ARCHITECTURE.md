# Paper Armies Book Architecture

## Status

This is a controlled architecture for the public book. It is not a manuscript,
not a proposal, and not a claim that the cases have been sourced.

## Working Title

Paper Armies: A Doctrine for Defense Reform in the Age of Speed, AI, and
Strategic Competition

## Reader Promise

The book explains how institutions can mistake representations for capability,
why local learning fails to travel, and how CALM can make claims carry proof
while helping valid learning move.

## Part I: Diagnosis - The Empire of Representation

Purpose: show what paper capability is before introducing doctrine.

1. The Map Outranks the Terrain: how representations become more trusted than
   reality.
2. The Green Signal: why visibility is not validity.
3. Ready for Which War?: why readiness without conditions becomes theater.
4. The Prototype Stage: why demonstration is not fielded capability.
5. The Interoperability Mirage: why connection is not coalition capability.
6. The AI Label: why artificial intelligence claims require operational
   receipts.
7. The Category Machine: how programs can succeed inside categories that no
   longer map to mission advantage.

## Chapter Packet Requirement

Each chapter packet must include:

1. Chapter thesis.
2. Opening scene.
3. Primary case.
4. Secondary case.
5. Countercase.
6. Sources.
7. CALM concept.
8. Incentive dynamic.
9. Overclaim guard.
10. Legal/security guardrail.
11. Implementation takeaway.
12. Memorable lines.

## Tone Rule

The book should be clinical enough to be defensible, readable enough to sell,
scathing enough to matter, and bridge-preserving enough to survive.

## Usefulness Check

This architecture prevents the book from opening with forms, formulas, or
backend formalism. It makes the public sequence reader-first: problem, cost,
cause, solution, application.

## Claim Boundary

This architecture does not source, draft, legally clear, or publish the book.
No chapter claim is allowed until its packet has source evidence and overclaim
guards.
