# Related Work Mapping

Status: draft map for review.

## Formal Logic

Paralogic must be compared against paraconsistent logic and related
non-explosive approaches. Current formal work should be described as
non-collapse modeling and typed semantic scaffolding unless a distinct
consequence relation is fully defined.

Source anchor: [SEP: Paraconsistent Logic](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/logic-paraconsistent/).

## Belief And Frame Change

Delta outcomes need comparison to belief revision, belief update, dynamic
epistemic logic, and model transformation. Current Delta artifacts are typed
outcome and policy structures, not a complete theory of rational belief change.

Source anchor: [SEP: Logic of Belief Revision](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/logic-belief-revision/).

## Institutions

ISFT's institutional predicates need grounding in social-institution theory.
Current formal objects are useful but thin: they encode institution/output/claim
relations without a full theory of institutional agency.

Source anchor: [SEP: Social Institutions](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/social-institutions/).

## AI Assurance And Risk

NIST AI RMF provides an external structure for risk management, trustworthy AI
attributes, and TEVV discipline. Paralogic should be mapped to these functions
without claiming compliance or validation.

Source anchors:

- [NIST AI RMF 1.0](https://www.nist.gov/publications/artificial-intelligence-risk-management-framework-ai-rmf-10)
- [NIST AI Risks and Trustworthiness](https://airc.nist.gov/airmf-resources/airmf/3-sec-characteristics/)

## Algorithmic Accountability And Auditing

The repair and bridge modules should be compared to algorithmic accountability
and ethics-based auditing literature, especially around opacity, standards,
stakeholders, and audit limitations.

Source anchors:

- [Data & Society Algorithmic Accountability Primer](https://datasociety.net/library/algorithmic-accountability/)
- [Ethics-Based Auditing of Automated Decision-Making Systems](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8260507/)
