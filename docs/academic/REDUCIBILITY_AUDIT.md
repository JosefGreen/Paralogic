# Reducibility And Non-Reducibility Audit

Status: internal audit.

## Reducibility Risks

1. Paralogic may reduce to known paraconsistent or relevance-logical machinery
   if its only distinctive feature is contradiction tolerance.
2. Delta calculus may reduce to belief revision/update theory if outcomes are
   merely belief-state transitions.
3. ISFT may reduce to an institutional accountability taxonomy if mechanisms
   are not given formal behavior beyond labels and profiles.
4. Repair calculus may reduce to ordinary audit remediation workflow unless
   its bridge/non-collapse structure proves useful.
5. Empirical operationalization may reduce to standard coding/reliability
   practice unless tied to the formal theorem ledger.

## Non-Reducibility Candidates

1. Explicit Lean-checked non-collapse families across formal, normative,
   empirical, and repair claims.
2. A staged pipeline that labels Lean, bounded finite search, custom proof
   checking, and empirical protocols with different statuses.
3. A single framework linking contradiction, insight, institutional support
   failure, normative bridges, repair, and empirical validation boundaries.
4. Machine-readable witnesses paired with formal theorem ledgers and
   anti-overclaim scans.

## Required Evidence Before Novelty Claim

- A systematic literature table with at least logic, belief revision,
  institutional theory, algorithmic accountability, auditing, and AI assurance.
- Expert review from at least one logician/formal methods reviewer and one
  sociotechnical/accountability reviewer.
- A written reducibility response for each high-risk overlap.
