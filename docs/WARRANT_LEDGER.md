# Warrant Ledger

## Purpose

This ledger prevents assumptions, supplied fields, operational schemas,
external sources, empirical validation, and review from collapsing into each
other.

CSV: `docs/ledgers/warrant_ledger.csv`.

## Scope

This is a benchmark warrant ledger for high-risk public claims and recurring
formal warrant patterns. It is not a final inventory of every warrant-like
phrase in the repository.

## Warrant Taxonomy

- W0_NOT_A_WARRANT
- W1_PRIMITIVE_INTERNAL
- W2_SUPPLIED_FIELD
- W3_OPERATIONALLY_DISCHARGED
- W4_COUNTERMODEL_GUARDED
- W5_EXTERNALLY_SOURCE_BACKED
- W6_EMPIRICALLY_SUPPORTED
- W7_REPLICATED_OR_EXTERNALLY_REVIEWED
- W8_UNSUPPORTED

## Main Findings

- Candidate mechanism profiles are mostly W2 supplied-field projections, even
  when their shape is Lean-checked.
- The operational core discharges selected predicates inside scoped models.
  It does not provide source backing, empirical support, or external review.
- Normative, legal, moral, repair, harm, and legitimacy conclusions require
  external warrants beyond the current repository.
- The safest public use is to block overclaiming, not to certify outcomes.

## Promotion Requirements

Promotion to source-backed requires cited sources and interpretation review.
Promotion to empirical requires data, methods, reliability evidence, construct
validity evidence, and replication. Promotion to reviewed requires documented
external expert review.

## Usefulness Check

This ledger helps a reader see which conclusions are supported only by internal
structure and which would require external evidence.

## Claim Boundary

This ledger does not prove that any warrant is externally sourced,
empirically validated, replicated, or externally reviewed unless explicitly
recorded as such with evidence.
