# Anti-Reward-Hacking Audit

## Executive Verdict

Verdict: MEDIUM risk, now actively bounded.

The repository has real formal and executable substance, but it has also grown
many ledgers, modules, and mechanism profiles. The main risk is mistaking
coverage, theorem count, or operational schemas for importance, source backing,
empirical validation, or public usefulness.

## Formal-Bloat Risk Inventory

M12 theory work is deferred for this benchmark. Adding another formal module
would not improve the immediate public-review outcome as much as claim
discipline, pilots, and ledgers.

## Theorem-Depth Inflation Risks

Projection theorems, `rfl` labels, and field extractions are useful regression
checks, but they are not substantive mathematical depth. The new theorem-depth
ledger marks representative examples as D0/D1 unless they block a false
inference, support a bounded checker, or establish a meta-theoretic result.

## Warrant-Promotion Risks

The highest-risk promotion is treating supplied warrant fields as derived
facts. The warrant ledger separates supplied, operational, source-backed,
empirical, and reviewed statuses.

## Market-Positioning Risks

AI governance, assurance, compliance, and audit language can invite
overclaiming. Current safe positioning is a research workbench for claim
boundary discipline, not a commercial assurance product.

## Practical-Usefulness Risks

The usefulness pilots are synthetic and initial. They show plausible utility in
claim auditing, not proven general utility.

## Simplifications Required

- Treat old pipeline language about total completion as historical or global,
  not as the current benchmark.
- Stop adding mechanism-specific theory until reviewer usefulness is improved.
- Prefer one worked example and one audit template over more abstraction.
