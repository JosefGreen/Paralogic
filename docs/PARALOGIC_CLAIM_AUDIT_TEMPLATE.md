# Paralogic Claim Audit Template

## Purpose

Use this worksheet to audit a policy memo, AI evaluation report, corporate
statement, public apology, school/workplace decision, or book argument.

## Worksheet

1. Claim being made:
2. Who is making the claim?
3. Who is affected?
4. Evidence offered:
5. Conclusion drawn:
6. Claim type: formal / empirical / normative / legal / moral / procedural /
   repair / evaluator / metric / symbolic.
7. Missing warrant:
8. Warrant status: primitive / supplied / operational / externally sourced /
   empirical / unsupported.
9. Does the evidence actually support the conclusion?
10. Is a symbol being treated as material repair?
11. Is a metric being treated as the value?
12. Is formal access being treated as substantive access?
13. Is evaluator acceptance being treated as truth?
14. Is legal or procedural compliance being treated as justice or legitimacy?
15. Is an apology or investigation being treated as accountability?
16. What counterexample would break the claim?
17. What safer conclusion is allowed?
18. What evidence would be required for a stronger conclusion?
19. Final claim boundary:

## Quick Mechanism Prompts

| risk | question |
| --- | --- |
| Evidence overclaim | Is evidence being used beyond its scope? |
| Metric proxy | Is a metric being treated as the target value? |
| Formal access | Is nominal access being treated as usable access? |
| Symbolic substitution | Is a symbol being treated as material change? |
| Repair failure | Is process being treated as completed repair? |
| Translation failure | Was meaning lost across context or audience? |
| Veto suppression | Could affected participants block or challenge the claim? |
| Frame drift | Did the claim move frames without continuity checks? |
| Symbolic overload | Does one symbol carry conflicting interpretations? |

## Usefulness Check

This template is designed to be usable without Lean. It helps a reader identify
missing warrants and safer conclusions.

## Claim Boundary

Completing this worksheet does not prove truth, wrongdoing, harm, illegality,
guilt, discrimination, coercion, legitimacy, or accountability.
